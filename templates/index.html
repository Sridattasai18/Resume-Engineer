Tips for the best results
  • Use action verbs: "Led", "Built", "Optimised"
  • Quantify everything: "40% faster" beats "improved speed"
  • Copy JD keywords directly into your bullets
  • Keep to one page — ATS often ignores page 2+
  • No tables, graphics, or fancy fonts
  • Paste the full JD, not just a summary

Questions

FAQ

▸Do you store my resume or job description?
No. Your data is sent directly to your chosen AI provider (Gemini or GPT) via your own API key. Nothing is stored on any server — each request is stateless.
▸Which template should I use?
Jake Classic (ATS 5/5) is the gold standard for CS/SWE roles globally. Datta Custom is best for Indian placements and PSUs. For ML/Data Science, try TLC Data Science.
▸Which AI provider gives the best results?
Both work well. GPT-4o tends to produce highly structured LaTeX and natural bullet points. Gemini 2.5 Flash is a good free option with a generous API quota.
▸How do I get a PDF from the LaTeX code?
Copy the LaTeX code → go to overleaf.com → New Project → Blank → paste → click Compile → Download PDF. Takes under a minute.
▸How accurate is the ATS simulation?
The AI simulates common ATS scoring: keyword density, format readability, section presence, and bullet clarity. Results are directionally accurate — not a guarantee, since each company's ATS differs — but give you strong, actionable signal.
▸Is this only for freshers / CS students?
No — it works for anyone. The section order and templates are optimised for fresher CS resumes by default, but the LaTeX mode works with any existing resume structure regardless of experience level or domain.
